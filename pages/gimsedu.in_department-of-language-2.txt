Overview
Department of Languages within an educational institution is responsible for offering a diverse array of language programs and courses, enabling students to develop proficiency in various languages while fostering an understanding of the associated cultures, literatures, and histories. These departments often provide language instruction, cultural studies, and may even include linguistics programs for the scientific study of language. They typically facilitate study abroad opportunities, engage in research, and may offer translation and interpretation courses. Additionally, they play a vital role in community outreach and provide valuable career opportunities for graduates across fields such as education, translation, international business, diplomacy, and journalism.

Our Vision
To be a globally recognized Department of Languages, fostering linguistic excellence, cultural understanding, and effective communication skills in our students.

Our Mission
Our mission is to provide a comprehensive and diverse language education that empowers students to excel in a multicultural world. We are committed to:

Academic Excellence: Delivering high-quality language education through innovative teaching methods and research-driven programs.

Cultural Understanding: Promoting cross-cultural awareness and appreciation through the study of languages and their associated cultures.

Communication Skills: Developing effective communication skills in multiple languages, both spoken and written.

Program Educational Objectives (PEOs):
Language Proficiency: Graduates will have a strong command of at least one foreign language and a deep understanding of its cultural context, enabling effective communication and cultural sensitivity in diverse settings.
Interdisciplinary Skills: Graduates will possess interdisciplinary skills that enable them to apply their language expertise in various professional fields, such as translation, diplomacy, international business, or education.
Translation and Interpretation: Students will be able to translate written texts accurately and provide interpretation services in professional settings.
Program Specific Outcomes (PSOs):
For the Department of Languages, specific learning outcomes might include:

Language Proficiency: Students will demonstrate proficiency in the target language(s) in terms of reading, writing, speaking, and listening comprehension.
Cultural Competence: Students will exhibit cultural competence by understanding and respecting the cultural norms, values, and practices of the communities associated with the languages they study.
Effective Communication: Students will demonstrate effective communication skills in both their native language and the target language(s).
Research and Analysis: Students will be able to conduct research on linguistic and cultural topics, critically analyze literature and texts, and apply their findings to various contexts.
